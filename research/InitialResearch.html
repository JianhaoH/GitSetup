Initial Research

Problem Statement

According to the National Library of Medicine, many patients avoid seeking treatment early on to circumvent a label of mental illness.Another study done by the NLM shows that only 2-11% of diagnoses are noted in medical record, and more than half of diagnosed cases go without treatment.

Need Statement

Negative public stereotyping of mental illnesses/PTSD make it hard for patients to seek help during the early stages of the illness.Organizations such as peer-based outreach programs and therapy groups may help PTSD patients resist the stigma and seek help.

  • “In primary care (PC) settings, patients with post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD), are often not diagnosed; 2% to 11% with PTSD actually have the diagnosis noted in the medical record. In addition, less than half of these patients with PTSD, or even fewer, actually receive treatment for PTSD”
  • "Common perceived stereotypes of treatment-seeking veterans with PTSD included labels such as "dangerous/violent," or "crazy," and a belief that combat veterans are responsible for having PTSD. Most participants reported avoiding treatment early on to circumvent a label of mental illness. Participants initially reported experiencing some degree of self-stigma; however, following engagement in treatment they predominantly resisted these stereotypes. Although most participants considered combat-related PTSD as less stigmatizing than other mental illnesses, they reported difficulties with reintegration. "
  • "Our findings have clear clinical applications. With fewer than 30% of sexual assault survivors ever seeking services for the psychological sequelae of trauma (George, Winfield, & Blazer, 1992; Golding, Siegel, Sorenson, Burman, & Stein, 1989) and with the lifetime prevalence of PTSD ranging as high as 50% to 80% for this group (Breslau et al., 1991; Kessler et al., 2005; Resnick, Kilpatrick, Dansky, Saunders, & Best, 1993), a need exists to identify women suffering from PTSD and to assist them to seek out a beneficial treatment.
